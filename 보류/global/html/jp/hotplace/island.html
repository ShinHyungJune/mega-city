Yanggu Korean Peninsula Island

First of all, before entering the Korean Peninsula, I would like to recommend that you see the intact Korean Peninsula Island. You can see the whole of the island by stopping at the Korean Peninsula Island Observation Deck located on the opposite side of the lake. When you arrive at the observatory and climb the stairs a little, you can take a panoramic view of Lake Faro and the Korean Peninsula. There is a telescope on the observatory, and if you look through the telescope, you can see the topography of the Korean Peninsula a little more accurately. However, it is highly recommended to capture the whole landscape with your own eyes rather than looking through a telescope. You can see an island shaped like a Korean peninsula at a glance. Nowadays, there is a rare opportunity to capture the sky, lakes, and green forests in your eyes, so you can feel the coolness that opens your mind as well as your eyes.

Once you've enjoyed the thrill with a zip line, it's time to go down to the Korean Peninsula. Walking along the wooden deck path that stretches toward the island of the Korean Peninsula feels like walking on the water. Yanggu is a place that boasts a variety of attractions no matter which season of the four seasons, but if you want to pick one of the best seasons to visit Lake Faro and the Korean Peninsula, I would definitely recommend visiting in autumn. The reason is the harmony between the sky and the lake. The unique coolness and clear sky of autumn, and the blue color of Lake Faro that illuminates the sky gives the impression of seeing a picture.

The calmness of the lake looks like a mirror reflecting the sky and mountains, and you can see the symmetrical view with the horizon at the center. I really want to express it with the words "blue is pouring out". The sky, the lake, and even the air feel blue. I want everyone to feel it at least once. Although it cannot solve everyone's worries, the story is a little different for anyone who captures this landscape. It seems that my frustrated mind is clearing and my head is clearing. In this place, you can lay down your worries and burdens of your mind for a while and feel the nature completely.

Another spectacular view is the sunset reflected on the lake at sunset. The tranquility of time when even the air sank and the inimitable lighting created by the slowly setting sun create a spectacular view. Walking with a loved one while looking at the lake, just imagination seems to give off a happy scent. This is where it is understood that the weather and the scenery can make people excited.

One more privilege for those visiting in the evening is that you can watch the musical fountain show in the middle of the lake. The musical fountain show starts every Friday, Saturday and Sunday from May to October at 8 pm. I hope you arrive a little early and walk while feeling the calmness of Lake Faro, and when the sun goes down, you can see the slightly contradictory and lively scenery of the musical fountain show and feel all the charms of the Korean Peninsula.

Really Halla to Baekdu?

Walking along the wooden deck road and walking into the island of the Korean peninsula, you can find the island with the Taegeukgi on it. Any Korean can think, "That's Dokdo!" You can also find Ulleungdo next to it. While admiring the details of the islands of the Korean Peninsula and Dokdo floating in the East Sea, I think that Dokdo and Ulleungdo should be located next to the Korean Peninsula. Once you have entered the Korean Peninsula in earnest, it is time to really feel "from Halla to Baekdu". You can decide where to start by looking around the place you want. If you passed Dokdo and Ulleungdo and entered the east coast, how about walking to the northern land of a place we have never been to? As I go north, I think, “Where is Pyongyang?” I imagine the land of North Korea that I haven't walked through, and I hope to get there someday. If you walk like that, you will have arrived at Baekdusan Mountain. Even in a dream, you can climb Mt. Baekdu in just a few steps. Once you have completed the trail of Mt. Baekdu, it's time to head south.

Think of the tranquil Lake Faro as the sea and the trees planted along the way as a forest.

You will now be able to reach Mount Jirisan. Jirisan is also fun to conquer in a few steps. Then it's time to leave Jeju Island. There are no boats or airplanes, but if you cross the bridge with two sturdy bridges, you will quickly arrive in Jeju Island. At the entrance of Jeju Island, Dolhareubang welcomes you as if this is Jeju Island. It really well captures the characteristics of the region, such as the traditional stone walls and gates of Jeju Island. Hallasan, a landmark of Jeju Island, rises in the middle and you can see Baeknokdam.

From Baekdusan Mountain to Hallasan Mountain, a certification shot is essential. It takes about an hour to tour the entire island, and an hour on the Korean Peninsula is enough time to run through the country. In addition, it is fun to create your own memories of traversing the country by taking pictures by finding different features of each region.

If you have seen enough inside of the Korean Peninsula, walk outside the island again to capture the landscape of Lake Faro and reflect on the excitement of the short length of the Korean Peninsula.
