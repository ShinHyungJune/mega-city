White Porcelain Museum

“The exclamation of white porcelain from the Goryeo period”

The white porcelain in Yanggu?

Among the things that come to mind when thinking about Yanggu, white porcelain will be hard to find. However, contrary to what we think, Yanggu white porcelain has a history as noble as its appearance. Yanggu has attracted attention as a porcelain production area since the Goryeo Dynasty. As a result of a survey of the Toyoji area in Yanggu, about 40 kiln sites were identified, of which about 7 regions identified the raw materials that were estimated to have been used to make white porcelain.

The white porcelain and blue and white porcelain collected in Yanggu are correlated with the porcelain of the late Joseon Dynasty. At the White Porcelain Museum, which shows the 600-year history of white porcelain in Yanggu, you can appreciate the lofty shape of Joseon white porcelain produced by Yanggu white clay, centering on the blue and white porcelain, the last flower of Joseon porcelain.

In July of this year, the Ceramic Culture History Office was opened. The newly expanded ceramic culture history room is largely divided into three spaces, and the first space is the storage room. It is an open storage warehouse that breaks from the existing closed structure and exhibits a part of the storage storage. Thanks to this, you can see the beauty of white porcelain from one step closer, such as entering the storage room, looking around the collection, and looking inside through a window. Here, I can feel the effort to enable the audience to communicate more actively.

The second space is the modern white porcelain room. This is a space where works made of white clay from Yanggu donated through events held at the museum are exhibited. You can see white porcelain that is simply "Made by Yanggu White Clay". You can also see the work of Australian potter Steve Harrison. It seems that the works of a foreign potter who made white porcelain, a traditional Korean porcelain from the soil of our country, are already enough to inspire curiosity.

The third space is the Ceramic History and Culture Room. This place is expressed in terms of “Yanggu white clay becomes a thousand lights”, and it is a place where Yanggu white clay is provided to 1,000 Korean potters and works are donated to exhibit. However, although 1000 lights have not yet been completed, it is interesting to see how this project, which continues through 2021, will be filled.

Since there are 1000 white porcelain that are all different, it seems that the light is already felt. An additional video room is indispensable, but this is a 5m high space with a size of 58 pyeong. What comes to mind at a glance in the video room shows the magic of a 360° panoramic large screen that makes the four-sided space appear on one side. If you are watching a video on this screen, you are not looking at the screen, but the ecstasy as if you exist in the space inside the screen.

If you are immersed in the beauty of white porcelain in a museum, you may suddenly think, "Can I have those white porcelain exhibited in the museum?" Because everyone wants to own beautiful things. So there is a space prepared. At the entrance of the museum, there is a museum shop where you can enjoy the beauty of white porcelain that you only saw with your eyes. You can purchase a variety of souvenirs made of soil from the Yanggu region, but if you want to feel the Yanggu white porcelain for a long time, we recommend looking at small items here.

It seems that enjoying the Yanggu White Porcelain Museum properly is not enough to just appreciate the white porcelain. Anyone may have imagined making pottery themselves, like in a movie scene. Making your own self by turning the wheel yourself to make the shape you want and engrave the picture you want to express.

If it feels a bit boring to immerse yourself in the history and beauty of white porcelain simply with your eyes, you can inspire a little by sharing various experiences prepared at the museum. It will be a time to soften the hardness of a museum, like clay that is touched by spinning a wheel.

Now, if you have seen various exhibitions of white porcelain inside the museum, and felt the porcelain with your five senses while putting the soil in your hands, why don't you come out and relax your body? Outside the Yanggu White Porcelain Museum, there are sculptures made of ceramics everywhere. It is fun to look inside the museum and find the hidden sculptures. Seeing porcelain sculptures made of porcelain in the soil and trees everywhere, you can feel not only the beauty of white porcelain, but also how well pottery harmonizes with nature. If you look for hidden sculptures, you will find yourself looking for a photo zone and leaving a commemorative photo.

If you want to feel the static Yanggu, the White Porcelain Museum is the perfect place to create history and elegance of white porcelain.
